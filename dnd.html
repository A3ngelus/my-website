Death Knight

  • Origin: The term Death Knight was formally introduced in Advanced Dungeons & Dragons (1st Edition, 1975).
  • Lore: Created by evil gods (often Demogorgon), death knights are fallen paladins who betrayed their oaths and were cursed with undeath.
  • Abilities: They can cast dark spells, control undead, and wield cursed weapons.
  • Purpose: Serve as high-level villains, symbolic of divine punishment and corruption.

Paladin

  • Created by Gary Gygax, based on Roland and the knights of Charlemagne. They were introduced in Dungeons & Dragons (1st Edition) as a Lawful Good holy warrior.
  • Unlike the Death Knight, the orignal paladin was designed to be a player character not a NPC, or non-playable character.
  • While they were supposed to be "Champions of justice, purity, and divine law" later editions gave the paladin more flexibility allowing players to have darker and/or more violent characters, examples of these include Oath of Vengeance and Oathbreaker paladins.
